# Exercise Reference Guide — How to Perform Each Exercise

Use this guide to learn how to perform each rehabilitation exercise. For more detail, follow the reference links (Physiopedia, NHS, or similar).

---

## 1. Squat
**How to:** Stand with feet shoulder-width apart. Bend your knees and push your hips back as if sitting into a chair. Keep your chest up and knees over toes. Go down until thighs are roughly parallel to the floor, then stand back up.
**References:** [Physiopedia - Squat](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Squat_Exercise) | [NHS Strength and flexibility](https://www.nhs.uk/live-well/exercise/)

## 2. Deep Squat
**How to:** Same as a squat but lower: bend knees and hips until your thighs are below parallel (deeper than 90 degrees at the knee). Keep back straight and core braced.
**References:** [Physiopedia - Deep Squat](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Deep_Squat)

## 3. Hurdle Step
**How to:** Step one foot over an imaginary hurdle (or low object). Lift the knee and place the foot in front, then step back. Repeat with the other leg. Stay balanced and upright.
**References:** [Functional movement - Hurdle Step](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Functional_Movement_Screen)

## 4. Inline Lunge
**How to:** Step one foot forward and lower your back knee toward the floor, keeping both knees at about 90 degrees. Front knee stays over the ankle. Return to stand and repeat on the other side.
**References:** [Physiopedia - Lunge](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Lunges)

## 5. Side Lunge
**How to:** Step one leg out to the side and bend that knee, keeping the other leg straight. Push your hips back. Return to centre and repeat to the other side.
**References:** [Side Lunge - Physiopedia](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Lunges)

## 6. Sit to Stand
**How to:** From a chair, shift forward slightly and stand up using your legs (minimal use of hands). Sit back down with control. Repeat. Good for leg strength and balance.
**References:** [NHS - Sit to stand](https://www.nhs.uk/live-well/exercise/physical-activity-guidelines-older-adults/) | [Physiopedia - Sit to Stand](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Sit-to_Stand)

## 7. Standing Leg Raise
**How to:** Stand on one leg. Slowly lift the other leg in front of you (or to the side for abduction) to about 45–90 degrees, then lower. Hold a wall or chair for balance if needed.
**References:** [Hip flexor / Leg raise](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Hip_Flexor_Stretch)

## 8. Shoulder Abduction
**How to:** With arms at your sides, raise both arms out to the sides and up to shoulder height (or as high as comfortable). Lower slowly. Keep shoulders relaxed.
**References:** [Physiopedia - Shoulder Abduction](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Shoulder_Abduction)

## 9. Shoulder Extension
**How to:** With arms at your sides or slightly in front, move them backward (behind the body) within a comfortable range. Squeeze shoulder blades gently. Return to start.
**References:** [Shoulder range of motion](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Shoulder_Range_of_Motion)

## 10. Shoulder Rotation
**How to:** Elbow bent 90 degrees at your side. Rotate your forearm and shoulder outward (external rotation) or inward (internal rotation) through a comfortable range.
**References:** [Rotator cuff exercises](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Rotator_Cuff_Exercises)

## 11. Shoulder Scaption
**How to:** Raise your arms in the “scaption” plane: about 30–45 degrees in front of your sides (between forward and side). Lift to shoulder height and lower.
**References:** [Scaption - Physiopedia](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Scapulohumeral_Rhythm)

## 12. Hip Abduction
**How to:** Stand on one leg (or lie on your side). Lift the other leg out to the side, keeping it straight. Lower with control. Often done lying down for stability.
**References:** [Hip Abduction - Physiopedia](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Hip_Abduction)

## 13. Trunk Rotation
**How to:** Seated or standing, rotate your upper body to one side from the waist, then to the other. Keep hips facing forward. Move slowly and within comfort.
**References:** [Spinal rotation](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Trunk_Rotation)

## 14. Leg Raise
**How to:** Lying on your back, keep one leg on the floor and lift the other toward the ceiling (or to 90 degrees). Lower slowly. Can also be done with both legs.
**References:** [Leg raise - core/hip](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Straight_Leg_Raise)

## 15. Reach and Retrieve
**How to:** Reach forward with one arm (e.g. toward an object or as far as comfortable), then bring the arm back. Repeat with the other arm. Good for shoulder and trunk mobility.
**References:** [Reach exercises - rehabilitation](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Reaching_Exercises)

## 16. Wall Push-up
**How to:** Stand arm’s length from a wall. Place palms on the wall at shoulder height. Bend elbows and bring your chest toward the wall, then push back. Easier than floor push-ups.
**References:** [Wall push up - Physiopedia](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Push_Up)

## 17. Heel Raise
**How to:** Stand with feet flat. Rise onto your toes, then lower slowly. Hold a wall or chair for balance. Strengthens calves and ankle stability.
**References:** [Calf raise - Physiopedia](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Calf_Raise)

## 18. Bird Dog
**How to:** On hands and knees, extend one arm forward and the opposite leg backward. Keep your back flat and core steady. Hold briefly, then switch sides.
**References:** [Bird dog - Physiopedia](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Bird_Dog_Exercise)

## 19. Glute Bridge
**How to:** Lie on your back with knees bent, feet flat. Lift your hips toward the ceiling, squeezing your glutes. Lower with control. Keep core engaged.
**References:** [Bridge exercise - Physiopedia](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Bridge_Exercise)

## 20. Clamshell
**How to:** Lie on your side with knees bent. Keep feet together and lift the top knee upward while keeping feet together. Lower and repeat. Targets hip external rotators.
**References:** [Clamshell - Physiopedia](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Clamshell_Exercise)

## 21. Chin Tuck
**How to:** Sit or stand with good posture. Gently tuck your chin straight back (as if making a “double chin”) without tilting the head down. Hold a few seconds, release.
**References:** [Chin tuck - neck](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Chin_Tuck)

## 22. Marching in Place
**How to:** March on the spot: lift knees toward hip height and swing arms naturally. Stay upright and land softly. Good for coordination and light cardio.
**References:** [Marching - balance & gait](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Balance_Exercises)

## 23. Step Up
**How to:** Step one foot onto a low step or platform. Push through that leg to bring the other foot up. Step down with control. Alternate legs. Use a step height that is comfortable.
**References:** [Step up - Physiopedia](https://www.physio-pedia.com/Step_Up_Exercise)

---

**Note:** Always warm up before exercise and stop if you feel pain. These are general guides; for a personalised programme, see a physiotherapist or doctor.
